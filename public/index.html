At KAIZN, we are Jan Piotraschke and Jan-Luca Klees, two realists dedicated to simplifying research and fostering continuous improvement, inspired by the Japanese concept of "kaizen."

We develop highly usable open-source software and hardware solutions that streamline existing processes, promote collaboration, and support researchers in their daily work. Our aim is to make scientific research more efficient, enjoyable, and accessible, ultimately leading to a more balanced life for those involved.

Our team values clarity, directness, respect, and personal growth. We encourage each member to become the healthiest and strongest version of themselves, focusing on self-guidance and responsibility for their results. Our distributed team works remotely, embracing flexibility and autonomy in the pursuit of our mission.

Our design philosophy is inspired by Zen gardens, emphasizing simplicity and clarity. We believe that a minimalist approach allows us to create streamlined solutions that seamlessly integrate with researchers' workflows. By embodying the principles of kaizen and Zen aesthetics.

We strive to transform the world of scientific research.

what we do

voy

project management

Our second software project, Voy, was developed in response to the challenges of managing a team of medical researchers. The data entry staff responsible for that was using ineffective tools like spreadsheets and postits to communicate open tasks with the medical researchers. With Voy, a ticekting system at it's core, we were able centralize these tasks, enabling real collaboration for the first time.

Currently, the first version of Voy, Ryoan-ji, is available for use and named after the renowned rock garden in Kyoto.

lucy

data streaming

Lucy is a research project that creates synthetic big data of tissue behavior using a physics engine. It all started with Jan's bachelor thesis, where we developed a exceptionally user-friendly interface for his complex simulation. We realized that there was a need for this type of software in the scientific community, and Lucy was born.

Today, Jan is working on his master thesis, and Lucy has evolved beyond recognition. The current work in progress is still under a different name.

dejima

data marketplace

Dejima is our vision of an innovative marketplace revolutionizing the way data is bought and sold. Our platform empowers scientists, researchers, and businesses to monetize their valuable data sets, unlocking new revenue streams while fueling groundbreaking discoveries.

By connecting data creators with data consumers, Dejima fosters collaboration, accelerates research, and promotes the exchange of high-quality datasets across various industries. All while prioritizing data privacy and compliance.
